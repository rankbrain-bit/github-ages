Virtual Cycling Apps

Taking up spinning is a way to keep fit that is hard to beat in terms of efficiency, affordability and satisfaction with the results. When done right, spinning raises your heart rate into the cardio zone, meaning your heart gets a thorough workout, and with time, noticeable improvements can be made to your overall health and fitness.

Aside from your heart health, making the time to incorporate regular spinning sessions into your weekly schedule makes it much easier to manage your weight, and can help you lose inches from your waist while sculpting strong, lean legs. People who take part in regular exercise, especially cardio, also reduce their chances of developing potentially debilitating conditions like diabetes, heart disease, stroke and osteoporosis.

To gain these fantastic benefits, you should aim to do at least an hour and a half of moderate exercise every week, preferably spread over three sessions. Of course, the more that you can safely do, the more strongly and quickly you will begin to experience these positive effects. Sustaining a regular schedule of exercise throughout your life means that you will carry these benefits into your more advanced years.

As we age, our hearts become less efficient and our resting heart rates increase which makes us vulnerable to the negative health impacts of aging and this is why people can tend to become obese and develop chronic health conditions. By setting yourself up for success in later life, most people can experience improved fitness levels as seniors, helping them to retain their independence and protecting their quality of life beyond retirement.

As spinning is not a weight bearing exercise, it is ideal for everyone at every stage of their lives, and provides an effective and gentle way to exercise which protects the joint of the knees, hips and ankles. This fact alone gives indoor cycling a distinct edge over other forms of cardio such as running, which can increase the rate of degeneration in the joints of the lower limbs.

Something that makes indoor cycling so appealing for many is that it can easily be accommodated in the home, especially when a more compact model of stationary bike is chosen. Your home spinning sessions will give the best results when they are done under the guidance of virtual cycling apps that monitor your progress and are packed with incredible features.

Many apps provide the rider with a library of classes to keep them on the path towards better performance, and you can even enjoy the thrill of live streamed group spinning classes from the comfort of your own home. These apps remove the need for expensive monthly gym fees and the best ones can even completely replace the services of a personal trainer through smart performance feedback and progress reports.

These apps are developed using the knowledge of health experts and professional athletes and are designed to provide a world-class spinning experience that is accessible and affordable, and is absolutely results-focused. Using a combination of health science and cutting edge tech, apps make use of your heart rate data to analyse your performance during every session so you can maximise the impact of your training.

At the core of this lies your heart rate zones and how you can move between them at intervals to ensure peak performance. Using this method provides a 20 minute workout that can be as effective as a whole hour spent pedal pushing. There are five heart rate zones in all, and you can and should make use of all of them in every session.

Starting with a warm up where your heart rate is only slightly elevated above your resting heart rate is the best way to ease into more intense activity, and from there you should build up to the cardio zone. You should aim to spend most of your session in the cardio zone, as this is where you will reap most of the health and fitness rewards for your efforts.

All of your sessions should end gradually with a gentle cool down period. Do not be tempted to neglect this step as it allows your heart rate and breathing rate to recover safely and helps to prevent any muscle cramps you may be experiencing after an intense session.
